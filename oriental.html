Apo Island Marine Sanctuary

One of the world's most celebrated marine protected areas, Apo Island is home to over 650 species of fish and 400 species of coral. But what makes it truly legendary is the sea turtles — specifically, the hawksbill and green turtles that have made this island their home for generations.

The sanctuary's success is entirely community-driven. In 1982, local fishermen voluntarily gave up fishing rights to one-third of the island's surrounding waters. Today, that sacrifice has turned Apo Island into a global model for marine conservation.

Turtle Sightings

Near-guaranteed encounters with green and hawksbill turtles on almost every dive or snorkel, year-round.

How to Get There

45-min bangka from Malatapay Beach Market (Zamboanguita), or day trips from Dumaguete. Overnight homestays available on the island.

Dumaguete City

City of Gentle People

Dumaguete is the capital of Negros Oriental — a university city with a distinctly calm, intellectual, and unhurried character.

The Heart of the City

Rizal Boulevard

The soul of Dumaguete is a 1.5-kilometre seafront promenade lined with acacia trees, pensiones, and open-air restaurants. At any hour, the Boulevard is alive — joggers at dawn, students with laptops by midday, families and couples at sunset.

The view across the narrow Tañon Strait frames the silhouette of Cebu Island in the distance. On clear evenings, the sky turns amber and violet — and the taho vendors make their rounds one final time.

"In Dumaguete, time does not move faster than a bicycle."

Manjuyod, Negros Oriental

Manjuyod Sandbar

The Maldives of the Philippines — a 7-kilometre stretch of white sand that emerges from the sea at low tide.

What it is

A 7km sandbar that lies submerged at high tide, then emerges as a glowing white strip of sand — ribboning across turquoise water with the mountains of Negros as backdrop.

The Stilt Cottages

Rustic bamboo cottages built on stilts in the middle of the sea. Wake up as the tide recedes and walk out onto your own private sandbar. Book well in advance during peak months.

Best Time to Visit

March to June for the longest, brightest sandbars. Visit at low tide (check tide tables before you go — timing is everything).

Dolphins on the Way

The boat ride from Bais City often passes through a dolphin corridor — spinner and spotted dolphins frequently accompany the bangka.

Twin Lakes of Balinsasayao

Two volcanic crater lakes — Balinsasayao and Danao — sit side by side in a protected natural park near Sibulan. Kayaks, forest trails, and morning mist over still water make this one of the most peaceful places in Oriental.

Est. 1901 · Dumaguete City

Silliman University

Founded by American Presbyterian missionaries, Silliman is the first American-founded university in Asia and the Philippines' first private university. More than a century later, its tree-canopied campus remains the intellectual and cultural nucleus of Dumaguete.

The campus itself is worth visiting — its Anthropology Museum, the landmark church, and the marine laboratory (one of the oldest in Southeast Asia) are all open to the public.

The Taste of Oriental

What to Eat in the South

Silvanas

Cashew meringue wafers sandwiched with French butter cream, then rolled in cookie crumbs. The definitive souvenir of Dumaguete — take home a box (or several).

Fresh Seafood

Grilled tuna, kinilaw (ceviche), and chilled crab from the Tañon Strait. The Malatapay market (Wednesday only) is where locals come for the freshest catch straight off the boats.

Budbud Kabog

Sticky millet wrapped in banana leaf and steamed with coconut milk — the traditional breakfast of the Orient. Paired with hot tsokolate (native cocoa), it's a morning that stays with you.
