Dauin, Negros Oriental

Apo Island
Marine Sanctuary

One of the world's most celebrated marine protected areas — home to over 650 species of fish and 400 species of coral. The sea turtles that have made this island their home for generations are near-guaranteed on every dive.

"In 1982, local fishermen gave up fishing rights to protect these waters. That act of faith turned Apo Island into a global model for marine conservation."
Turtle Sightings

Near-guaranteed encounters with green and hawksbill turtles on almost every dive or snorkel, year-round.

How to Get There

45-min bangka from Malatapay Beach Market (Zamboanguita). Day trips or overnight homestays available.

Dumaguete City

City of Gentle People

A university city with a distinctly calm, intellectual, and unhurried character — the capital of Negros Oriental.

🎓

The Heart of the City

Rizal Boulevard

The soul of Dumaguete is a 1.5-kilometre seafront promenade lined with acacia trees, pensiones, and open-air restaurants. At any hour, the Boulevard is alive — joggers at dawn, students with laptops by midday, families at sunset.

The view across the narrow Tañon Strait frames the silhouette of Cebu Island in the distance. On clear evenings, the sky turns amber and violet — and the taho vendors make their rounds one final time.

"In Dumaguete, time does not move faster than a bicycle."
Manjuyod, Negros Oriental

Manjuyod Sandbar

The Maldives of the Philippines — a 7-kilometre stretch of white sand that emerges from the sea at low tide.

What it is

A 7km sandbar submerged at high tide, emerging as a glowing ribbon of white across turquoise water — the mountains of Negros as backdrop.

Stilt Cottages

Rustic bamboo cottages on stilts in the middle of the sea. Wake up as the tide recedes and walk out onto your own private sandbar.

Best Time to Visit

March to June for the longest sandbars. Visit at low tide — check tide tables before you go. Timing is everything.

Dolphins on the Way

The boat ride from Bais City passes a dolphin corridor — spinner and spotted dolphins frequently accompany the bangka.

Twin Lakes of Balinsasayao

Two volcanic crater lakes — Balinsasayao and Danao — sit side by side in a protected natural park near Sibulan. Kayaks, forest trails, and morning mist over still water make this one of the most peaceful places in Oriental.

Est. 1901 · Dumaguete City

Silliman University

Founded by American Presbyterian missionaries, Silliman is the first American-founded university in Asia and the Philippines' first private university. More than a century later, its tree-canopied campus remains the intellectual and cultural nucleus of Dumaguete.

The campus is worth visiting on its own — the Anthropology Museum, the landmark church, and the marine laboratory (one of the oldest in Southeast Asia) are all open to the public.

The Taste of Oriental

What to Eat in the South

Silvanas

Cashew meringue wafers sandwiched with French buttercream, rolled in cookie crumbs. The definitive souvenir of Dumaguete — take home a box (or several).

Fresh Seafood

Grilled tuna, kinilaw, and chilled crab from the Tañon Strait. The Malatapay market (Wednesdays only) is where locals come for the freshest catch straight off the boats.

Budbud Kabog

Sticky millet wrapped in banana leaf, steamed with coconut milk — the traditional breakfast of the Orient. Paired with hot tsokolate, it's a morning that stays with you.
