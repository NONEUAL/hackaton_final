The Heart of the Visayas

Negros Island — officially divided into Negros Occidental and Negros Oriental — is the fourth-largest island in the Philippines. Known as the Sugar Capital of the Philippines, its fertile plains have fueled the nation's sweet heritage for over a century.

From the volcanic highlands of Mt. Kanlaon to the pristine reefs of Apo Island, Negros offers an extraordinary range of landscapes, cultures, and flavors that reward every kind of traveler.

The island is home to the legendary MassKara Festival — the City of Smiles in full celebration — and the gentle, university-town charm of Dumaguete, where life flows easy along the boulevard.
