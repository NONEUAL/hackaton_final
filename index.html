The Philippines' Visayas

The Heart of the Visayas

Negros Island — divided into Negros Occidental and Negros Oriental — is the fourth-largest island in the Philippines. Known as the Sugar Capital of the Philippines, its fertile plains have fueled the nation's sweet heritage for over a century.

"From the volcanic highlands of Mt. Kanlaon to the pristine reefs of Apo Island — the island rewards every kind of traveler."

Home to the legendary MassKara Festival and the gentle, university-town charm of Dumaguete, where life flows easy along the boulevard.

Talisay City, Negros Occidental

The Ruins of Talisay

Standing amid sugarcane fields, The Ruins is the skeletal remains of the early 20th-century mansion of Don Mariano Ledesma Lacson — built as a monument of love for his Portuguese wife, Maria Braga, who died in childbirth.

During World War II, retreating Filipino guerrillas burned the mansion rather than surrender it to Japanese forces. The Italianate columns survived the flames, earning it the nickname "Taj Mahal of Negros."

Open daily, 8am–10pm Talisay City Entrance fee applies

Murcia, Negros Occidental

Mambukal Mountain Resort

Nestled at the foot of Mt. Kanlaon, Mambukal is a government-run mountain resort featuring seven waterfalls, natural hot and cold pools, and jungle trails winding through primary forest.

At dusk, thousands of giant fruit bats emerge from the forest canopy — a surreal natural spectacle you won't forget.

7 waterfalls Hot sulfur springs Murcia

Silay City, Negros Occidental

Silay Heritage City

Known as the "Paris of Negros," Silay City is home to 30+ heritage mansions from the Spanish and American colonial eras — the finest concentration of ancestral houses in the Philippines.

The Balay Negrense (1897) is the crown jewel — a well-preserved Victorian-style house that now serves as a museum of Negrense hacienda life.

30+ heritage houses Walking tour available

Canlaon City, Negros Occidental

Mt. Kanlaon Natural Park

At 2,465 meters, Mt. Kanlaon is the highest peak in the Visayas and an active stratovolcano. The surrounding natural park is one of the Philippines' most important biodiversity hotspots.

Home to the Negros bleeding-heart pigeon and Philippine cockatoo — the mossy forests at upper elevations feel utterly otherworldly.

2,465m summit Active volcano Permit required

Dauin, Negros Oriental

Apo Island Marine Sanctuary

One of the world's most celebrated marine protected areas, Apo Island is home to over 650 species of fish and 400 species of coral. Sea turtle encounters are near-guaranteed year-round.

In 1982, local fishermen voluntarily gave up fishing rights to one-third of the surrounding waters. Today, Apo Island is a global model for community-driven marine conservation.

650+ fish species 45-min bangka from Malatapay Homestays available

Manjuyod, Negros Oriental

Manjuyod Sandbar

A 7-kilometre stretch of white sand that lies submerged at high tide, then emerges as a glowing ribbon of sand across turquoise water — with the mountains of Negros as backdrop.

Rustic bamboo cottages on stilts let you wake up as the tide recedes and walk out onto your own private sandbar. The boat ride from Bais City often passes spinner and spotted dolphins.

Best: March–June Visit at low tide Stilt cottages

Capital, Negros Oriental

Dumaguete City

The "City of Gentle People" — a university city with a distinctly calm, intellectual, and unhurried character. Home to Silliman University (est. 1901), the oldest American-founded university in Asia.

Rizal Boulevard is the city's soul: a 1.5-km seaside promenade lined with acacia trees, open-air restaurants, and views across the Tañon Strait to Cebu.

Silliman University 1901 Rizal Boulevard Sans Rival Cakes

Valencia, Negros Oriental

Casaroro Falls

A dramatic 30-meter single-plunge waterfall hidden at the end of a canyon trail through bamboo forest. The hike involves ladders and river crossings — proper footwear required.

A natural swimming hole at the base of the falls offers a refreshing reward after the trek.

30-meter drop ~1hr hike Valencia

Negros Oriental · Delicacy

Silvanas

Cashew meringue wafers sandwiched with French buttercream, then rolled in cookie crumbs. The definitive frozen souvenir of Dumaguete — best eaten straight from the freezer.

Sans Rival Café, Dumaguete Keep frozen

Negros Occidental · Heritage Product

Muscovado Sugar

Unrefined raw cane sugar with a rich, deep molasses flavor — the soul of Negros' sugarcane heritage. Unlike refined white sugar, muscovado retains its natural minerals and color.

Used in traditional Negrense sweets like piaya and pastillas, and increasingly sought by artisanal bakers worldwide.

Bacolod public markets Best pasalubong from Occidental

Negros Occidental · Food

Chicken Inasal

Bacolod's gift to Philippine cuisine. Chicken marinated in calamansi, vinegar, lemongrass, and annatto, then grilled slowly over charcoal. Eat it with garlic rice drizzled in chicken oil.

Manokan Country, Bacolod Charcoal grilled

Negros Occidental · Pasalubong

Piaya

A sweet, flaky flatbread filled with muscovado sugar — Bacolod's most beloved take-home snack. Baked on a flat griddle until golden, with a slightly chewy, caramelized center.

Sold in packs El Ideal Bakery, Silay

A decadent dessert of crispy cashew-studded meringue layered with rich French buttercream. The name means "without rival" — a claim the Dumaguete version fully earns.

Bacolod City · October

MassKara Festival

Born in 1980 as a defiant act of joy after two tragedies — the sinking of MV Don Juan and the collapse of sugar prices — MassKara means "many faces" and celebrates the Negrense spirit of resilience.

The festival runs the entire month of October, climaxing on the weekend nearest the 19th.

October (peak: 3rd weekend) Bacolod City Book 2 months ahead

Bacolod City · April

Panaad sa Sugbo

A grand provincial showcase held at the Panaad Park and Stadium in Bacolod. Each municipality sets up pavilions featuring local food, crafts, cultural performances, and tourism highlights.

April Panaad Park, Bacolod

Dumaguete City · October

Buglasan Festival

The "Festival of Festivals" — a week-long celebration in Dumaguete that gathers the mini-festivals of Negros Oriental's various municipalities into one grand showcase.

October Dumaguete City

Dumaguete City · November

Sandurot Festival

"Sandurot" means to gather together — perfectly capturing the spirit of this inclusive, community-centered festival held to commemorate Dumaguete's foundation anniversary.

November Dumaguete City

Bacolod City · October

Inasal Festival

Held alongside the MassKara celebrations in October, the Inasal Festival is a food-lovers' paradise dedicated entirely to Bacolod's iconic grilled chicken. Cook-offs, grill competitions, and street feasts.

October Grill competitions

Dauin, Negros Oriental · July

Apo Island Dive Festival

An annual celebration of marine conservation centered around Apo Island. Features underwater photography contests, reef cleanups, and guided dives with marine biologists.

July Dauin / Apo Island Photo contest
