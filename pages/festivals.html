Bacolod City · October

MassKara Festival

MassKara was born in 1980 as a conscious, defiant act of collective joy. Two devastating events struck Negros Occidental that year: the sinking of the passenger ferry MV Don Juan in the Tablas Strait, which killed hundreds of Negrenses, and the collapse of global sugar prices, which devastated the province's entire economy. City leaders responded not with mourning but with a festival — "MassKara," meaning "many faces," as a declaration that the people of Bacolod would smile through everything.

The festival has grown from that defiant origin into one of the Philippines' most celebrated annual events. The centerpiece is the street dancing competition: thousands of performers in elaborate smiling-mask costumes and vibrant costumes fill the streets of Bacolod in synchronized choreography, competing for provincial and national titles. The masks themselves — each hand-painted and decorated — are judged separately in mask-making competitions that run alongside the dancing.

Highlights & Schedule
  • Street dancing competitions — the main event, 3rd weekend of October
  • Mask-making competition — judged separately from dancing
  • Trade fair, food festival, and concerts throughout October
  • Electric MassKara — an evening street party and rave event
WhenOctober (peak: 3rd weekend)
WhereBacolod City proper, city plaza
Origin Year1980 — 45+ years of celebration
TipBook accommodation 2 months ahead
Bacolod City · April

Panaad sa Sugbo

Panaad sa Sugbo — literally "vow on the island" in Hiligaynon — is Negros Occidental's grand annual provincial festival, held at the Panaad Park and Stadium complex in Bacolod City every April. Each of the province's 31 municipalities and 13 cities sets up an individual pavilion showcasing its own local identity: unique food products, traditional crafts, cultural performances, and tourism attractions.

For visitors, Panaad is essentially a one-stop tour of the entire province in a single park. In the space of a few hours, you can taste kakanin from Kabankalan, watch a bamboo-weaving demonstration from Himamaylan, see folk dances from Cadiz, and sample the muscovado products of La Carlota. The agricultural exhibits, cultural shows, and trade fairs run simultaneously throughout the week-long event, making every day a different experience.

Highlights
  • 31 municipal pavilions — a complete tour of Negros Occidental
  • Agricultural exhibits and food product showcases
  • Cultural dance competitions and live performances
  • Trade fair with local artisans and entrepreneurs
WhenHoly Week / April annually
WherePanaad Park & Stadium, Bacolod
DurationOne week
EntranceFree or minimal gate fee
La Castellana · January

Sinulog sa Kanlaon

Sinulog sa Kanlaon is Negros Occidental's own celebration of the Feast of the Santo Niño — a devotional festival held in January in the municipality of La Castellana, at the foot of Mt. Kanlaon. The festival takes its name from the sinulog dance: a rhythmic two-steps-forward, one-step-back movement that mimics the flow of river water and is traditionally performed as an act of devotion to the Child Jesus.

While the Cebu Sinulog is far more famous, the Kanlaon version carries a distinctly Negrense character — smaller in scale, more community-driven, and set against the backdrop of one of the Philippines' most dramatic volcanoes. Religious processions wind through the town's streets in the early morning hours, followed by street dancing competitions, cultural presentations, and the traditional fluvial parade along local waterways.

Highlights
  • Sinulog street dancing — two-steps-forward devotional movement
  • Religious procession honoring the Santo Niño
  • Mt. Kanlaon as the dramatic natural backdrop
  • Community-scale festival — intimate and authentic
WhenThird Sunday of January
WhereLa Castellana, Negros Occidental
SignificanceDevotion to the Santo Niño
VibeIntimate, community-driven, devout
Negros Occidental · August

Sugarcane Industry Week

Sugarcane Industry Week is an annual celebration of the industry that has defined Negros Occidental for over four centuries — held every August to coincide with the peak of the harvest season. The event is organized by the Sugar Regulatory Administration alongside the provincial government and the major sugar planters' associations, and brings together farmers, millers, researchers, traders, and consumers in a week-long acknowledgment of sugar's central role in the province's economy and identity.

The public-facing components of the event include product exhibitions where muscovado, tablea, piaya, and other sugar-derived products are showcased and sold, agricultural trade fairs where new sugarcane varieties and farming technologies are demonstrated, and cultural programs celebrating the hacienda heritage and the lives of sugarcane workers — the sacadas — who have been the backbone of this industry for generations.

Highlights
  • Muscovado and specialty sugar product exhibitions
  • Agricultural trade fair and new variety demonstrations
  • Cultural programs honoring the sacada workers
  • Sugar mill tours and hacienda open days
WhenAugust annually
WhereBacolod City & provincial venues
FocusIndustry, heritage, and agri-tourism
Best ForBuying artisanal sugar products
Bacolod City · October

Inasal Festival

The Inasal Festival is Bacolod's dedicated celebration of its most famous culinary export — Chicken Inasal — held annually in October alongside the MassKara festivities. While MassKara celebrates the Negrense spirit through dance and art, the Inasal Festival celebrates it through food: the smoky, charcoal-grilled chicken that has become the most recognized dish in the Philippines after the adobo.

The festival centers on Manokan Country — the legendary strip of open-air inasal stalls in Bacolod — which transforms during the event into a cook-off arena and street feast venue. Professional and amateur grill masters compete in timed inasal cooking competitions judged on marinade, grill technique, and presentation. The public component is a street feast where thousands of visitors eat inasal, garlic rice, and chicken oil at long communal tables along the city's main streets.

Highlights
  • Inasal cook-off competition — open to professional and amateur grillers
  • Street feast at Manokan Country — communal dining at its best
  • Chicken oil and sinamak-making demonstrations
  • Held alongside MassKara — combine both for the full Bacolod October experience
WhenOctober (MassKara week)
WhereManokan Country, Bacolod City
Don't MissThe cook-off finals on the main stage
Pairs WithMassKara Festival (same week)
Dumaguete City · October

Buglasan Festival

The Buglasan Festival is Negros Oriental's answer to MassKara — and its own kind of masterpiece. Known as the "Festival of Festivals," Buglasan gathers the distinctive mini-festivals of Negros Oriental's many municipalities into one grand week-long showcase held in Dumaguete every October. Each participating municipality brings its own festival identity — its own costumes, dances, music, and cultural story — and performs before judges and spectators in the provincial capital.

The effect is extraordinary: in a single week, you witness the Tambayon Festival of Tanjay, the Kasadyaan Festival of Canlaon, the Pintaflores of San Carlos, and dozens more — a living atlas of Negros Oriental's cultural diversity. The street dancing competitions are the centerpiece, but Buglasan also encompasses food exhibitions, product showcases, and cultural forums that explore the province's history and heritage in depth.

Highlights
  • Street dancing competition — all municipalities competing simultaneously
  • A living showcase of every distinct mini-festival in the province
  • Food and product exhibitions from each municipality
  • Cultural forums, heritage talks, and museum events
WhenOctober (3rd week)
WhereDumaguete City, Negros Oriental
DurationOne week
Meaning"Buglasan" = to converge / come together
Dumaguete City · November

Sandurot Festival

The Sandurot Festival commemorates the founding anniversary of Dumaguete City and celebrates the city's identity as a place of friendship, openness, and cultural exchange. "Sandurot" comes from the Visayan word meaning "to gather together" or "to meet" — a fitting name for a city that has historically welcomed students, scholars, expats, and travelers with remarkable warmth.

The festival's character reflects the city it celebrates: more cultural and intellectual than purely spectacular, with as much emphasis on heritage exhibitions, academic symposia, and community gatherings as on the street parade and dancing competitions. The Rizal Boulevard — the soul of Dumaguete — becomes the stage for the main parade, with the Visayan Sea as backdrop and the old bell tower as the anchor of the procession route.

Highlights
  • Foundation Day parade along Rizal Boulevard
  • Cultural performances and heritage exhibitions
  • Silliman University events and academic forums
  • Community gatherings celebrating Dumaguete's welcoming identity
WhenNovember (Foundation Anniversary)
WhereDumaguete City, Rizal Boulevard
Meaning"Sandurot" = to gather together
VibeCultural, community, intellectual
Tanjay City · December

Tambayon Festival

The Tambayon Festival is Tanjay City's annual celebration of its identity as a fishing and agricultural community on the shores of the Tañon Strait. "Tambayon" refers to a traditional fishing vessel used by local fishermen in the strait — the humble bangka that has fed the community and connected it to the sea for generations. The festival honors this maritime heritage through street dances, folk art displays, and coastal ceremonies.

Held every December to coincide with Tanjay City's foundation anniversary, the Tambayon Festival has grown into one of Negros Oriental's most anticipated provincial celebrations. The street dancing performances feature elaborate sea-themed costumes — fish, waves, coral, and bangka motifs — choreographed to music that incorporates traditional Visayan instruments alongside contemporary arrangements.

Highlights
  • Sea-themed street dancing with intricate bangka-inspired costumes
  • Coastal ceremonies honoring the fishing heritage of Tanjay
  • Folk art exhibition featuring traditional fishing implements
  • Fresh seafood feast and local product showcase
WhenDecember (Foundation Anniversary)
WhereTanjay City, Negros Oriental
ThemeFishing heritage and maritime culture
Named AfterThe traditional tambayon fishing vessel
Bindoy · March

Daing Festival

The Daing Festival of Bindoy is one of the most charmingly specific festivals in the Philippines — a celebration built entirely around daing, the traditional Filipino dried and salted fish that is a staple of every Filipino breakfast table. Bindoy, a coastal municipality on the eastern shore of Negros Oriental, has been one of the leading producers of artisanal daing in the Visayas for generations, and its annual festival celebrates that identity with pride and considerable humor.

The festival takes place every March and features a street dancing competition where dancers perform in costumes made from or inspired by dried fish — silver sequins, fish-scale patterns, and sun-drying racks feature prominently in the choreography. Beyond the dancing, the festival is a true food event: daing cooking competitions, product exhibits, and a community feast where Bindoy's finest dried fish products are available for tasting and purchase.

Highlights
  • Daing-themed street dancing — one of the Philippines' most unique costumes
  • Dried fish cooking competition with traditional and modern recipes
  • Community feast — the freshest daing you'll ever eat
  • Product exhibit and direct sales from local producers
WhenMarch annually
WhereBindoy, Negros Oriental
ThemeDried fish industry and coastal identity
Best ForAuthentic local culture + great seafood
Dauin · July

Apo Island Dive Festival

The Apo Island Dive Festival is an annual celebration of marine conservation held every July in Dauin, the jumping-off point for Apo Island. Founded as a partnership between the local dive industry, the Apo Island community, and Silliman University's Institute of Environmental and Marine Sciences, the festival combines competitive events with genuine conservation work — making it one of the few festivals in the Philippines where attending is also an act of environmental stewardship.

The centerpiece events are an underwater photography competition — attracting professional and amateur underwater photographers from across Southeast Asia — and a reef cleanup dive organized by dive operators, conservation organizations, and volunteers. Educational forums on marine biology, reef conservation, and sustainable tourism round out the program, often featuring scientists from Silliman University's marine laboratory who have been studying the Apo Island ecosystem for decades.

Highlights
  • Underwater photography competition — open to all skill levels
  • Reef cleanup dive — organized and supported by local dive shops
  • Marine biology forums with Silliman University researchers
  • Guided conservation dives with briefings on reef ecology
WhenJuly annually
WhereDauin & Apo Island, Negros Oriental
Best ForDivers, photographers, conservationists
RegisterVia local Dauin dive operators
