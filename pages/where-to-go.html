Talisay City · Negros Occidental

The Ruins of Talisay

Standing amid the sugarcane fields of Talisay, the Ruins is the skeletal remains of the early 20th-century mansion of Don Mariano Ledesma Lacson — built as a monument of love for his Portuguese wife, Maria Braga, who died in childbirth.

During World War II, retreating Filipino guerrillas torched the mansion rather than surrender it to Japanese forces. The coral-and-egg-white walls survived, earning it the nickname "Taj Mahal of Negros."

Highlights
  • Sunset viewing — the golden hour turns the ruins amber and orange
  • Italian Renaissance–inspired architecture and surviving columns
  • Surrounding sugarcane gardens and lily pond
  • On-site restaurant and souvenir shop
LocationTalisay City, Negros Occidental
HoursDaily, 8:00 AM – 10:00 PM
Entrance Fee₱100 adults / ₱50 children
From Bacolod~15 minutes by tricycle or car
Murcia · Negros Occidental

Mambukal Mountain Resort

Nestled at the foot of Mt. Kanlaon, Mambukal is a government-run mountain resort spanning over 24 hectares of primary forest with seven named waterfalls and natural hot and cold sulfur pools.

At dusk, thousands of giant fruit bats emerge from their roosts in a sweeping, darkening sky — one of the most dramatic natural spectacles in Occidental.

Highlights
  • Seven waterfalls — each with a distinct character and trail difficulty
  • Natural hot and cold sulfur spring pools
  • Giant fruit bat colony — spectacular evening flight at dusk
  • Camping grounds, cottages, and zip line
LocationMurcia, Negros Occidental
HoursDaily, 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM
Entrance Fee₱50 adults / ₱30 children
From Bacolod~1 hour by jeepney via Murcia
Silay City · Negros Occidental

Silay Heritage City

Known as the "Paris of Negros," Silay City is home to over 30 ancestral houses from the Spanish and American colonial eras — the finest and most concentrated collection of heritage architecture in the Philippines.

Highlights
  • Balay Negrense Museum — most visited heritage house in Negros
  • 30+ ancestral houses within walking distance
  • San Diego Pro-Cathedral (1934)
  • El Ideal Bakery — serving Napoleones since 1920
LocationSilay City, Negros Occidental
Best ForHeritage walking tours, photography
Museum Fee₱50–₱100 per heritage house
From Bacolod~30 min north by bus or car
Canlaon City · Negros Occidental

Mt. Kanlaon Natural Park

At 2,465 meters above sea level, Mt. Kanlaon is the highest peak in the Visayas and one of the Philippines' most active stratovolcanoes, sheltering dozens of endemic species.

Highlights
  • Highest summit in the Visayas at 2,465 meters
  • Endemic species: Negros bleeding-heart pigeon, Philippine cockatoo
  • Ancient mossy cloud forests above 1,800m elevation
  • Three summit trails of varying difficulty
LocationCanlaon City, Negros Occidental
PermitRequired — apply via DENR
DifficultyStrenuous — experienced hikers only
Duration2 days / 1 night summit climb
Canlaon City · Negros Occidental

Mt. Kanlaon Summit Trail

The summit trails of Mt. Kanlaon pass through some of the most extraordinary landscapes in the Philippines — from lowland forest through mossy cloud forest to the stark, windswept crater ridge.

Highlights
  • Three established trails: Guintubdan, Mapot, and Wasay
  • Views across both the Visayan Sea and Sulu Sea on clear days
  • Makawiwili Ridge — the active crater area
  • Giant tree ferns and ancient mossy forest at mid-elevation
Best TrailGuintubdan — most established route
PermitRequired — apply via DENR
GuideMandatory — arrange in Canlaon City
SeasonMarch–May best; avoid typhoon season
Dauin · Negros Oriental

Apo Island Marine Sanctuary

In 1982, the local fishing community gave up fishing rights around one-third of the island's reef — creating a no-take zone that became a global model for grassroots marine conservation.

Highlights
  • Sea turtle encounters — near-guaranteed year-round
  • 650+ fish species and 400+ coral species
  • Pristine wall dives and snorkel spots
  • Community-run homestays and local dining
LocationDauin, Negros Oriental
Getting There45-min bangka from Malatapay Beach
Best SeasonYear-round; calmest March–June
StayHomestays available on the island
Manjuyod · Negros Oriental

Manjuyod Sandbar

A 7-kilometre stretch of pristine white sand that lies submerged at high tide, then slowly emerges as the waters recede — a glowing ribbon of pale sand against impossible turquoise.

Highlights
  • 7-km white sandbar — widest at low tide (March–June)
  • Dolphin-watching on the boat ride from Bais City
  • Stilt cottages for overnight stays on the sandbar
  • Snorkeling, kayaking, and sandbar picnics
LocationManjuyod, Negros Oriental
Getting ThereBoat from Bais City port
Best TimeMarch–June (lowest tides)
TipCheck tide charts before booking
Valencia · Negros Oriental

Casaroro Falls

A 30-meter single-plunge waterfall hidden at the bottom of a narrow canyon in the mountains above Valencia. The trail involves descending more than 300 steps and crossing a rocky stream several times.

Highlights
  • 30-meter single-drop waterfall in a narrow canyon
  • Cathedral-like acoustics and atmosphere at the base
  • Bamboo forest trail with river crossings
  • Cold, deep plunge pool for swimming
LocationValencia, Negros Oriental
From Dumaguete~25 min by habal-habal to trailhead
Hike Duration~45 min round trip
GearWater shoes / sturdy sandals essential
Valencia · Negros Oriental

Casaroro Falls Trail

The bamboo canyon trail to Casaroro Falls is an adventure in itself — a descent through dense forest, over stepping stones and rope bridges, before the roar of the falls announces your arrival.

Trail Info
  • Over 300 steps cut into the canyon walls
  • Multiple stream crossings — expect wet feet
  • Bamboo groves and hanging bridges along the route
  • Short but steep — allow extra time for the return climb
DifficultyModerate — steps are slippery when wet
Duration45 min–1 hour round trip
Best TimeDry season (Dec–May)
EntranceSmall fee collected at trailhead
Manjuyod · Negros Oriental

Manjuyod Stilt Cottages

Rustic bamboo stilt cottages built directly over the sandbar — you fall asleep to the sound of the sea and wake up surrounded by water, with the sandbar slowly reappearing beneath your feet as the tide retreats.

Overnight Experience
  • Cottages sit directly over the water at high tide
  • Sunrise views over the Bohol Sea — completely unobstructed
  • Fresh seafood meals prepared by the caretakers
  • No electricity — bring a power bank and embrace the stars
Book ViaBais City tourism office
CapacityLimited cottages — book ahead
IncludesBoat transfer + meals (usually)
Best TimeMarch–June for widest sandbar
