Negros Oriental · Signature Delicacy

Silvanas

Silvanas are the undisputed signature pasalubong of Dumaguete — a frozen dessert-cookie hybrid perfected at Sans Rival Cakes and Pastries on Perdices Street. Two crisp cashew meringue wafers sandwich a generous layer of French-style buttercream, rolled in fine cookie crumbs.

They are best eaten straight from the freezer — the buttercream firms to a consistency somewhere between ice cream and frosting, and the wafers stay perfectly crisp.

Ingredients
Cashew nutsEgg whitesSugarButterVanillaCookie crumbs
OriginDumaguete City, Negros Oriental
Best Bought AtSans Rival Café, Perdices St.
StorageKeep frozen at all times
Negros Occidental · Iconic Dish

Chicken Inasal

Chicken Inasal is Bacolod's most celebrated contribution to Philippine cuisine. The dish is marinated in a blend of calamansi juice, vinegar, lemongrass, ginger, and annatto oil, then grilled slowly over charcoal.

Served with garlic rice drizzled with chicken oil and sinamak (spiced vinegar), eating it at Manokan Country is the authentic experience — everything else is a reference.

Key Ingredients
ChickenCalamansiCane vinegarLemongrassAnnatto
OriginBacolod City, Negros Occidental
Must Try AtManokan Country, Bacolod
Served WithGarlic rice, chicken oil, sinamak
Negros Occidental · Heritage Product

Muscovado Sugar

Muscovado is unrefined raw cane sugar from which the molasses has not been separated — the result is a dark, moist, deeply flavored sugar with a caramel-toffee richness. Negros Occidental produces the majority of the Philippines' sugar output.

It is the essential ingredient in piaya, pastillas, and many traditional Negrense sweets, and has recently found an international market as a premium baking ingredient.

OriginNegros Occidental province
Where to BuyBacolod public markets, pasalubong centers
Negros Occidental · Pasalubong

Piaya

Piaya is a thin, round, flaky flatbread stuffed generously with muscovado sugar — baked on a hot griddle until the surface is golden and the filling has caramelized into a chewy, molasses-rich center. It is the single most popular pasalubong from Bacolod.

Ingredients
All-purpose flourMuscovado sugarShorteningWaterSalt
OriginBacolod City, Negros Occidental
Best Bought AtEl Ideal Bakery, Silay City
Negros Oriental · Signature Dessert

Sans Rival Cake

Sans Rival — French for "without rival" — is a layered Filipino dessert cake consisting of multiple layers of crisp cashew-studded dacquoise meringue sandwiched with rich French-style buttercream. The Dumaguete version, made by Sans Rival Cakes and Pastries, is considered the gold standard.

Ingredients
Cashew nutsEgg whitesSugarButterEgg yolksVanilla extract
OriginDumaguete City, Negros Oriental
Best Bought AtSans Rival Café, Perdices St.
Negros Occidental · Festival Craft

MassKara Festival Mask

The MassKara mask is the most iconic symbol of Bacolod City — a perpetually smiling face adorned with bright flowers, sequins, mirrors, and colorful embellishments representing the Negrense spirit of resilience and joy.

Festival-grade masks are wearable works of art: hand-painted, hand-decorated, and often incorporating real dried or silk flowers, rhinestones, and intricate filigree patterns unique to each maker.

OriginBacolod City, Negros Occidental
Where to BuyBacolod public market, SM Bacolod
Negros Occidental · Native Delicacy

Banana Pinasugbo

Banana Pinasugbo is one of Negros Occidental's most beloved native sweets — thin slices of saba banana deep-fried and then glazed in a thick, bubbling muscovado sugar syrup until they emerge amber-colored, glossy, and irresistibly crunchy.

The name itself says it all: pinasugbo means "dipped in sugar syrup" in Hiligaynon. The combination of the banana's natural starchiness with the deep caramel richness of muscovado creates a candy-like snack that is impossible to eat just one of.

Ingredients
Saba bananaMuscovado sugarCooking oilWater
OriginNegros Occidental province
Where to BuyLibertad Market & pasalubong shops, Bacolod
Negros Occidental · Heritage Snack

Barquillos

Barquillos are thin, crispy, lightly sweetened wafer tubes — rolled by hand while still hot from a flat iron griddle into their signature cylindrical shape. Their name comes from the Spanish barquillo, meaning "small boat" or "wafer roll," reflecting Negros' Spanish colonial culinary heritage.

In Bacolod, barquillos are often sold stuffed with a loose filling of toasted pinipig or enjoyed plain as an afternoon snack with a glass of tsokolate.

Ingredients
All-purpose flourSugarEggsButterVanilla
OriginBacolod City, Negros Occidental
Where to BuyLibertad Market & El Ideal Bakery, Silay
Negros Occidental · Bakery Classic

Biscocho

Biscocho — from the Spanish bizcocho, meaning twice-baked bread — is one of the oldest and most enduring baked goods in the Philippines, and Bacolod's version is considered among the finest in the country.

The Bacolod biscocho is distinguished by its generous butter coating and the deep caramelization of its sugar glaze. It pairs perfectly with hot coffee or tsokolate, and keeps well for weeks — making it ideal as a long-shelf-life pasalubong.

Ingredients
Bread (pan de sal base)ButterSugarGarlic (savory variant)
Variants
  • Sweet biscocho — buttered and sugar-glazed, the classic version
  • Garlic biscocho — savory, brushed with garlic butter
  • Often sold alongside piaya as a paired pasalubong set
OriginBacolod City, Negros Occidental
Best Bought AtBiscocho Haus, Bacolod City
Shelf Life2–3 weeks in an airtight container
Negros Island · Sustainable Craft

Upcycled Decor

Negros Island's upcycled décor movement transforms agricultural and industrial waste into striking handcrafted home furnishings and decorative objects. Artisans across both provinces work with sugarcane bagasse, coconut shells, abaca fiber, repurposed wood from old hacienda structures, and discarded festival materials to create pieces that carry the island's history in their very material.

The sugarcane connection is especially significant: bagasse — the fibrous pulp left after cane juice is extracted — is woven, molded, and treated to create everything from lampshades to picture frames. What was once industrial waste becomes an object of beauty that tells the story of the island's sugar heritage.
