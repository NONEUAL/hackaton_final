Talisay City, Negros Occidental

The Ruins
of Talisay

Standing amid the sugarcane fields of Talisay, the Ruins is the skeletal remains of the early 20th-century mansion of Don Mariano Ledesma Lacson — built as a monument of love for his Portuguese wife, Maria Braga, who died in childbirth.

"The walls glowed gold in the sunset — like the love story that built them was still burning."

During World War II, retreating Filipino guerrillas burned the mansion rather than surrender it to Japanese forces. The Italianate columns and coral-and-egg-white walls survived — earning it the nickname "Taj Mahal of Negros."

The Hacienda Life

Sugarbowl of
the Philippines

For over two centuries, the rhythm of life in Occidental has been set by the sugarcane harvest. The hacienda system — vast estates managed by landed families — shaped the island's culture, architecture, cuisine, and soul.

Every piaya, every muscovado-dusted pastry, every glass of basi wine — all carry the memory of the fields.

October · Bacolod City

MassKara Festival

1980 — The Birth
Born from Crisis

Two tragedies struck in 1980: the sinking of MV Don Juan and the collapse of sugar prices. City leaders created MassKara as a defiant act of joy — MassKara meaning "many faces."

The Masks
The Icon of Smiles

Each elaborately painted smiling mask is handcrafted by local artisans. Performers compete in street dancing with increasing intensity as the festival week builds to its grand finale.

How to Attend
The Grand Parade

The festival runs the entire month of October, climaxing on the weekend nearest the 19th. Book accommodation at least 2 months in advance — the whole island converges on Bacolod.

The City of Smiles

Bacolod City

Bacolod is the beating heart of Occidental — a city that prides itself on warmth, food, and an almost defiant cheerfulness. Beyond the festival, there's a city worth wandering slowly.

01
Manokan Country

A strip of open-air inasal stalls that have been grilling since the 1970s. The smoke, the communal dining, the liver sauce — this is the real Bacolod experience.

02
Bacolod Capitol Park & Lagoon

The civic heart of the city — a grand Art Deco-influenced capitol building overlooking a lagoon, where locals jog, children feed ducks, and couples watch the sunset.

03
Calea Pastries & Coffee

The most famous bakery in Negros — possibly in the Visayas. Known for its brazo de mercedes and layered cakes. Expect a queue; it's always worth the wait.

04
San Sebastian Cathedral

A Spanish colonial cathedral at the center of Bacolod, where the faithful gather every morning and the pigeons own the plaza by afternoon.

A Taste of the North

Flavours of Occidental

Chicken Inasal

The smoky, savory soul of Bacolod. Marinated in calamansi, vinegar, lemongrass, and annatto — grilled over charcoal, eaten with garlic rice drizzled in chicken oil.

Piaya & Napoleones

The definitive Bacolod pasalubong. Piaya is a flatbread stuffed with muscovado sugar; Napoleones are flaky cream-filled pastries from Silay. Buy boxes of both.

Calea's Famous Cakes

Some of the finest bakery work in the country. The brazo de mercedes — a rolled meringue and custard cake — is legendary. The queues start before the doors open.
